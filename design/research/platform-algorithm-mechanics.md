# Platform Algorithm Mechanics — Deep Research Document

**Research date:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Data-driven reference for AI Campaign Studio V2 content optimization
**Methodology:** Aggregated from Buffer (7.1M posts), Sprout Social (2.7B engagements / 463K profiles), Socialinsider, Statista, and multiple platform-specific studies.

## 1. TikTok Algorithm Ranking Signals

### Signal Hierarchy (Weighted Point System)

Widely reported point system (not officially confirmed by TikTok, but consistent across reverse-engineering studies):

| Signal | Points | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Rewatch / Replay | 5 | Highest |
| Watch to completion | 4 | Very High |
| Share | 3 | High |
| Save | 3 | High |
| Comment (meaningful) | 2 | Medium |
| Like | 1 | Lowest |

**Key insight:** Watch time is the #1 overall signal, accounting for ~40-50% of algorithmic weight. A video's completion rate matters more than raw view count.

### Completion Rate Thresholds

- **2024:** ~50% completion rate needed for viral distribution
- **2025-2026:** ~70% completion rate needed for viral distribution (raised significantly)
- Videos with 70%+ completion get roughly **3x more reach** than those below threshold
- "Qualified Views" (>5 seconds watch time) now gate distribution — videos failing this metric die at 200 views

### Other Ranking Factors (2026)

- **Original audio:** Receives 3x more algorithmic weight than trending sounds (December 2024 change)
- **TikTok SEO:** Keywords in captions, on-screen text, and spoken audio are scanned for search ranking
- **Comment quality:** Length and depth of comments now matter more than comment count
- **Follower engagement:** Since late 2025, follower response is central to distribution — videos are shown to followers first, then expand if followers engage

### Oracle Algorithm Transfer (US)

- Deal closed January 22, 2026 — Oracle now operates and retrains the US algorithm
- Algorithm retraining began Q1 2026, full transition expected Q2 2026
- Early reports: FYP quality noticeably changed; historical performance data may become less predictive
- Expected shift toward stricter content moderation and advertiser-friendly content prioritization

## 2. YouTube Shorts Algorithm

### How It Differs from TikTok

| Factor | TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
|---|---|---|
| Primary signal | Watch time / completion | Swipe-away rate / completion |
| CTR matters? | Somewhat | No (users swipe, don't click) |
| Recency bias | Yes (first hours critical) | No (can go viral weeks later) |
| Search integration | Growing (TikTok SEO) | Strong (dedicated Shorts search filter, Jan 2026) |
| Max length | 10 min | 3 min (raised from 60s, Oct 2024) |
| Algorithm coupling | Single algorithm | Fully decoupled from long-form (late 2025) |

### Key Ranking Signals

1. **Swipe-through rate** — Most important. How many people stop to watch vs. swipe away
2. **Watch duration / completion** — Shorts with >70% completion promoted more aggressively
3. **Loop/replay rate** — Rewatches signal strong interest
4. **Shares** — Carry particular weight as indicator of distribution-worthy content
5. **Likes, comments, subscribes** — Standard engagement signals

### Shorts Shelf & Distribution Model

- **Test-and-expand model:** Seed audience watches → if positive signals → expanded to larger pool → repeat
- Each successful round triggers ~5-10x audience expansion
- A Short can go from hundreds to millions of views within hours if engagement holds at each expansion
- **Unlike TikTok, timing matters less** — the algorithm evaluates Shorts over hours and days, not just the first few minutes

### Scale

- YouTube Shorts: **200 billion daily views** (announced mid-2025) — up 186% from 70B in March 2024

## 3. Instagram Reels Algorithm

### Ranking Signals (Confirmed by Adam Mosseri, Jan 2025)

**The Big Three:**

1. **Watch time** — #1 ranking factor for initial distribution
2. **Likes per reach** — Matters more for existing follower distribution
3. **Sends per reach (DM shares)** — Most heavily weighted signal for new audience distribution

**Critical data point:** A Reel with 50 DM shares and moderate likes will outperform one with 500 likes and zero shares.

### The 3-Second Rule

- Instagram heavily weighs whether viewers continue watching past the first 3 seconds
- Reels with **>60% 3-second hold rate** outperform those with <40% by **5-10x in total reach**

### Distribution Process

1. Shown to random **non-followers first** to gauge broader interest
2. If non-followers engage → distributed to your followers
3. If followers engage → pushed to Explore for wider reach

This is the opposite of TikTok's 2026 model (which now shows to followers first).

### 2025-2026 Updates

- **"Your Algorithm" feature (Dec 2025):** Users can see and edit topic categories used for Reel recommendations. Reels matching user-selected topics saw reach jump **40-50%**
- **"Originality Score":** Detects recycled clips. TikTok watermarks or reused trending content significantly tanks reach
- **Trial Reels:** Feature allowing creators to test content with non-followers before showing to own audience

### Engagement Benchmarks

- 694,000 Instagram Reels are sent via DM **every minute**
- Reels reach ~36% more users than carousels and 125% more than photo posts
- Average Reels engagement rate: ~1.23-5.53% (varies by measurement methodology)

## 4. Optimal Posting Times (Data-Backed)

### TikTok — Buffer Study (7.1M Posts Analyzed, 2026)

**Best overall slots:** Sunday 9 AM, Monday 1 PM, Sunday 1 PM (EST)

| Day | Best Times (EST) |
|---|---|
| Monday | 7 AM, 11 AM, 10 PM |
| Tuesday | 6 AM, 7 AM, 10 PM |
| Wednesday | 6 AM, 9 PM, 10 PM |
| Thursday | 6 AM, 1 PM, 10 PM |
| Friday | 6 PM, 8 PM, 10 PM |
| Saturday | 11 AM, 7 PM, 8 PM |
| Sunday | 7 AM, 9 AM, 4 PM |

**Pattern:** Evening hours (6-11 PM) generally peak. Afternoons (12-5 PM) lowest engagement most days. Saturday is the top-performing day overall.

**Critical context:** The first 60 minutes after posting determine ~80% of a video's success, because TikTok tests content with a small group during that window.

### Instagram — Sprout Social (2.7B Engagements, 463K Profiles, 2025)

- **Best overall window:** Monday-Thursday, 10 AM - 3 PM
- **Best single day:** Wednesday (broad engagement window 11 AM - 9 PM)
- **Monday peak:** 1-2 PM
- **Retail-specific:** Monday 11 AM & 1-3 PM, Thursday 2 PM

### YouTube Shorts

- **Best days:** Tuesday-Thursday (Wednesday strongest)
- **Best windows:** 12-3 PM and 7-10 PM (audience local time)
- **Standout slot:** Tuesday/Wednesday at 4 PM
- **Important:** Timing matters LESS for Shorts than other platforms — algorithm evaluates over hours/days

### Posting Frequency (Diminishing Returns Data)

**TikTok (Buffer, 11M+ posts):**
- Sweet spot: **2-5 posts per week** — most meaningful lift in views
- 1-2 times daily: 2-3x higher per-video engagement than 4+ times daily (Later study)
- Going from 1 to 2-5/week gives the best ROI per additional post
- 11+/week brings biggest absolute lift but worst efficiency per post
- Posting 4+ daily causes audience fatigue and unfollows

## 5. Video Length Optimization

### TikTok

| Duration | Best For | Data |
|---|---|---|
| 11-18 seconds | Maximum virality | Highest completion rates, fastest viral spread |
| 21-34 seconds | Storytelling | 1 in 4 most-liked TikToks fall in this range |
| 15-30 seconds | Overall sweet spot | Highest completion + share rates |
| 90-120 seconds | Views + engagement | Highest raw view counts and engagement numbers |
| 1-3 minutes | Long-form push (2026) | Algorithm now boosting longer storytelling content |

**Hook rule:** 63% of highest-CTR videos highlight their main message within the first 3 seconds.

### YouTube Shorts

| Duration | Performance |
|---|---|
| Under 10 seconds | Poor — 22x fewer views than 50-60s Shorts |
| 15-20 seconds | Good for single ideas, quick tips |
| 20-35 seconds | Versatile sweet spot for most niches |
| 35-60 seconds | Best for tutorials, stories, transformations |
| 50-60 seconds | Highest raw view counts (Inflow Network, 5,400 Shorts studied) |

### Instagram Reels

| Duration | Performance |
|---|---|
| 7-15 seconds | Highest completion rate + shareability (CreatorsJet, 500 viral Reels) |
| 15-30 seconds | Best overall engagement |
| 60-90 seconds | Good for deeper content, still promoted for discovery |
| Over 90 seconds | Algorithm deprioritizes for discovery |

**Key insight across all platforms:** Shorter videos have higher completion rates (which feeds the algorithm), but mid-length videos often get higher absolute engagement numbers. Optimize for completion if you want reach; optimize for depth if you want engagement quality.

## 6. The Initial Push — First Distribution Mechanics

### TikTok

- **Initial test pool:** 200-500 viewers in the first 1-3 hours (primarily non-followers matching your content's topic)
- **Minimum threshold:** ~50 engagement points needed to advance past test phase
- **Scaling:** Each successful wave multiplies audience by ~5-10x
  - Wave 1: 300-500 viewers
  - Wave 2: 2,000-5,000 viewers
  - Wave 3: 10,000-50,000 viewers
  - Each subsequent wave continues if engagement holds
- **"200-view jail":** Videos that fail to generate enough Qualified Views (>5 seconds) or have low completion rate stop at ~200 views
- **2026 change:** Videos now shown to followers first → if followers engage well, expands to non-followers → if non-followers engage, goes wider

### YouTube Shorts

- **Test-and-expand model** — no specific pool size publicly documented
- Seed audience → positive signals → 5-10x expansion → repeat
- Can spike from hundreds to millions within hours
- **Key difference:** Algorithm evaluates over longer time horizon — a Short can resurge days or weeks later

### Instagram Reels

- **Shows to non-followers first** (opposite of TikTok's 2026 model)
- Tests broader interest before pushing to your followers
- Performance in first hour matters but slow starters aren't necessarily dead
- **Trial Reels feature** lets you explicitly test with non-followers before full publish

## 7. Shadowban / Algorithmic Suppression Triggers

### Common Triggers Across All Platforms

1. **Community guidelines violations** — Even "borderline" content (nearly breaks rules) gets demoted without formal notice
2. **Competitor watermarks** — TikTok logo on Instagram Reels = suppressed. Instagram watermark on TikTok = suppressed
3. **Engagement manipulation** — Bot-style engagement, buying followers/likes
4. **Behavioral anomalies** — Sudden activity spikes after dormancy, rapid IP/device changes
5. **Spam-like posting** — Same hashtags on every post, excessive posting volume
6. **Certain sounds/hashtags** — Some can trigger automatic content suppression
7. **Duplicate/recycled content** — Low "Originality Score" on Instagram

### Platform Terminology

Platforms don't use "shadow ban" officially. They call it "recommendability" — content simply isn't recommended to others. It's a trust-based distribution system that silently throttles reach.

### Duration

- Typical: **few days to two weeks**
- Severe cases: several weeks to months
- Recovery: stop violating behavior, wait, post high-quality original content

### Research Data

- Mozilla Foundation (2022): 70% of users flagged for "hate speech" by AI moderators on YouTube were **misclassified** — highlights false positive risk in automated systems

## 8. Hashtag Effectiveness

### Do They Help? Yes — With Caveats

- TikTok videos using relevant hashtags get **>2x more views**
- Instagram posts with at least 1 hashtag: **+29% interactions** (accounts <1K followers)
- Instagram posts with 11+ hashtags: **+79.5% interactions** (accounts <1K followers)

### Optimal Number (2025-2026 Consensus)

| Platform | Optimal Count | Why |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 3-5 | Fewer tags prevent mixed algorithmic signals (per CapCut/ByteDance) |
| Instagram | 3-5 | Shift from old "use 30" advice; algorithm penalizes spam behavior |
| YouTube Shorts | 3-5 in description | Functions as SEO keywords |

### Strategy: Trending vs. Niche

- **Mix both:** 1-2 broad/trending tags (#FYP, #viral) + 2-3 niche-specific (#BookTok, #HomeWorkout)
- Niche hashtags drive higher-quality, more engaged viewers
- Trending hashtags provide volume but lower engagement quality
- **2025-2026 shift:** Hashtags now function as **search keywords** (TikTok SEO) rather than mass reach tools
- Using the same 30 tags on every post may flag your account as automated/low-effort

## 9. Cross-Posting Penalties

### The Verdict: No Cross-Platform Detection, But Watermarks Kill You

- **Platforms do NOT communicate with each other** — no cross-platform duplicate detection
- **Cross-posting itself does NOT get penalized** and increases total reach
- **The ONLY penalty is watermarks** — every platform actively suppresses content with competitor watermarks
  - TikTok watermark on Instagram Reels = crushed
  - Instagram watermark on TikTok = suppressed
  - Remove watermarks before cross-posting and content performs normally

### Platform-Specific Notes

- **Instagram:** "Originality Score" detects recycled clips, but "original" means "you created it," not "it's exclusive to Instagram"
- **Facebook (Meta):** Announced in 2025 it would push down "unoriginal" or duplicated content patterns — wants content created FOR Facebook
- **TikTok:** Rewards native uploads — upload directly rather than via third-party APIs when possible
- **YouTube Shorts:** No documented penalty for cross-posted content

### Best Practice

Download without watermarks → re-upload natively to each platform → adjust captions/hashtags per platform. This is free reach multiplication, not a penalty risk.

## 11. Engagement Velocity

### How Fast Do Early Interactions Need to Happen?

**TikTok:**
- First 60 minutes = ~80% of a video's success determination
- Initial test pool (200-500 viewers) evaluated in first 1-3 hours
- First 3 seconds of watch time are critical for retention signal
- Must generate enough "Qualified Views" (>5 seconds) to pass initial gate

**Instagram Reels:**
- Engagement velocity measured in first **30-60 minutes** after posting
- 3-second hold rate is the critical early metric
- >60% hold rate at 3 seconds = 5-10x more total reach than <40%
- DM shares in first hour carry disproportionate weight
- Peak velocity windows: 7-9 PM evenings, weekend mornings

**YouTube Shorts:**
- Less time-pressured than TikTok/Instagram
- Algorithm evaluates over hours and days, not just first minutes
- However, early swipe-away rate still matters — if seed audience bounces, expansion doesn't happen
- Can resurge days or weeks later unlike other platforms

### The Velocity Formula

**Speed of engagement matters as much as volume.** 50 shares in the first 30 minutes signals more to algorithms than 500 shares spread over a week. This is why posting time matters — you need your content hitting eyeballs when your audience is most active and most likely to engage quickly.

## 12. Leaked / Reverse-Engineered Algorithm Details

### TikTok Internal Mechanics

- **No officially leaked source code or internal documents** as of March 2026
- The point system (Rewatch=5, Completion=4, Share=3, Comment=2, Like=1) is widely reported but not officially confirmed by TikTok
- One researcher spent 6 months reverse-engineering with 50 fake accounts and 100,000+ videos, validating the hierarchical signal model
- Academic research (Shin & Jitkajornwanich, 2024, published in Social Science Computer Review) used reverse engineering to study how algorithms promote self-radicalization

### Known Internal Mechanics

- **Traffic pool algorithm:** New content enters test pool of 100-500 views
- **Minimum engagement threshold:** ~50 engagement points needed to advance to next distribution tier
- **Negative signals actively tracked:** "Not Interested" taps, video skips before 5 seconds, hiding content from a creator
- **Device/account signals used:** Device type, language preference, country setting, content language all factor into initial distribution targeting

### What Platforms Have Officially Disclosed

- **TikTok:** Published "How TikTok recommends videos #ForYou" blog post explaining general principles but no numerical weights
- **Instagram:** Mosseri has done multiple public Q&As naming specific signals (watch time, likes/reach, sends/reach)
- **YouTube:** Creator Insider channel and official blog discuss signals in general terms; YouTube Studio provides audience activity data
